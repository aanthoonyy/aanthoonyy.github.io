Unit 1 Reflections

1. How do you define "inclusion?" What does it mean for an environment to be inclusive?

To me inclusion means a space were people of all groups and backgrounds have access to the same opportunites and resources. For an environment to be able to provide that space, it must have values rooted in respecting differences that people might have from another person. Provide the same opportunites to all people, and support all people in whatever they want from their own life. Everyone should feel safe from bullying and harassment, and everyone should have an opportunity learn about other peoples views to better understand each other.

What do you think are the particular benefits of online environments for inclusion?

I think the particular benefits of online environments for inclusion is that it allows you to connect with people who aren't normally people you can connect with. I think about how movements can spread so fast over social media or how there are niche communties of people that you can meet from all over the world. I like cars, and I've been fixing up my Mazda Miata over the last couple years, and the online Miata community allowed me to get rare parts, help diagnose, and even make some friends in the greater PNW. And I know there are a lot of communities for a lot of different things someone could be into.

What do you think are the particular challenges in online environments for inclusion?

I think the particular challenegs in online environments for inclusion is that there is a little bit of anonymity. Which can be a good thing maybe, but it can allow for people to harass and bully people without reprocussions in their real lives. Another thing that can be seen as a negative could be issues with censorship, I think a some countries don't have free access to the internet, which could suppress groups of people.
